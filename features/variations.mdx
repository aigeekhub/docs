The Variations engine lets you take any artifact and instantly see it rendered in a completely different visual aesthetic — without changing a single word of its content or a single unit of its layout logic. Choose from more than 25 style presets, pick a theme, and Ai Studio produces three distinct reskins using Google Gemini's structured output capabilities.

## What are variations?

Variations are style-preserving reskins. When you generate a variation, the engine:

1. **Reads the structure and content** of your focused artifact — its components, copy, hierarchy, and interactive logic.
2. **Applies a new visual aesthetic** defined by your chosen style preset and theme — new color palettes, typography, surface treatments, shadows, borders, and motion cues.
3. **Returns three conceptual variations** of that aesthetic, each a slightly different interpretation, so you still get meaningful choices.

The original artifact is never modified. Variations produce new artifacts that you can apply, discard, or iterate on independently.

## How to generate variations

<Steps>
  <Step title="Focus an artifact">
    Click on any artifact card to bring it into focus. The header controls will update to reflect the focused artifact's available actions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the variations panel">
    Click **NEURAL VARIATIONS** in the header. The Variations panel slides open alongside the artifact.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a style preset">
    Select one of the 25+ style presets from the style picker. Hover over any style to see a brief description of its aesthetic character.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a theme">
    Select **Dark**, **Light**, or **Custom** from the theme selector. Custom lets you describe specific color and tone requirements in free text.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate">
    Click **Generate**. Ai Studio calls Gemini with structured JSON output constraints to produce three variation artifacts in parallel. Each one streams into its own card in the variations panel.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Themes

Themes set the broad tonal context that the style preset operates within:

- **Dark** — deep backgrounds, light foreground text; ideal for high-contrast and atmospheric styles like Cyberpunk or Dracula.
- **Light** — bright backgrounds, dark foreground text; works well with Minimalist, Swiss Modern, and Corporate Memphis.
- **Custom** — enter a free-text description of the tonal environment you want. For example: *"Warm sunset tones, amber background, dark brown text."*

The theme is layered on top of the style preset, so **Glassmorphism + Dark** and **Glassmorphism + Light** will produce distinctly different outputs.

## Applying a variation

Once your three variation cards have rendered:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review the three variations">
    Scroll through the variation cards. Click any card to preview it full-size in an interactive iframe.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Apply your preferred variation">
    Click **APPLY DNA** on the card you want to keep. This replaces the currently focused artifact with the selected variation, creating a new session state. The original artifact is preserved in your session history.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Continue iterating">
    Your applied variation is now a first-class artifact. You can generate further variations from it, enter [Revision Mode](/features/revision), or [export it](/features/export).
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Variations preserve the original artifact's **business logic and content** — every button, form field, data point, and label carries over unchanged. Only the visual presentation layer is reskinned. If you need to change structure or copy, use [Revision Mode](/features/revision) after applying a variation.
</Note>
